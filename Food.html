Eating in the City

Chicago is known for being a top rated city to eat and drink in. The city has even won awards for our tasty food, such as being rated the food and drink capital of the world according to a global survey via "Time Out Index". Here are a few of my favorite places to eat in the city!

For Breakfast, I enjoy going to Batter and Berries to indulge in their world famous french toast! Batter & Berries resides in the Lincoln Park neighborhood of the city with an address of 2748 N Lincoln Ave, Chicago, IL 60614. The doors to Batter & Berries opened up back in 2012 by Dr. Tanya & Craig Richardson who had the passion to add an eclectic world class restaurant to brunch scene in the city.The popular breakfast spot has been serving up creative offerings, such as decadent French toast flights and deconstructed omelets, and so much more. While Batter & Berries is most known for their take on breakfast food items, the restaurant also serves Lunch and Dinner. Pugh III Chicagoans from all walks of life who stop in for a break, a good cup of coffee and great community-focused conversations. Known for its exceptional quality and flavor, Sip & Savor coffees are certified fair trade, and in some cases, certified organic and shade grown. Be sure to stop by and have a sip!

To satisfy your cold sweet tooth craving, head on over to Shawn Michelle's Homemade Ice Cream in the Bronzeville neighborhood on any given afternoon. I'm sure that you'll find a flavor combination fit for every mood. My favorite flavor here is the cookies and cream ice cream. Here is the scoop on Shawn Michelle's, the sweet spot is located at 46 E 47th St, Chicago, IL 60653 and offers homemade ice cream including a vegan menu!

Truth Italian Restaurant is another favorite restaurant of mine and they specialize in both their dinner and brunch menu. The founder, Peytyn Willborn, built the restaurant on the visions of a “world class kitchen that produced five-star Italian Cuisine for everyday people” and to bring an elegantly designed business with great service to the Southside of Chicago, located in the heart of Bronzeville. Truth prides themselves on having an intimate layout designed to make its patrons comfortable and provide a social atmosphere for the local Chicago foodie who doesn’t want to travel far for a sophisticated experience with great food and even better prices. Make sure to check out Truth Italian the next time you are in Bronzeville.

To satisfy your sweet tooth craving, go on over to my favorite Donut shop known as “Dat Donut”’. Dat Donut has been a Chatham neighborhood staple since 1994 and they are most known for their infamous “Big Dat” donut. The owners, Darryl and Andrea, opened their flagship location on the corner of 83rd Street and Cottage Grove Avenue. The team at Dat Donut takes pride in a good quality product and friendly service and their bakers have combined experience exceeding 25 years specializing in fresh and delicious hand-cut donuts.
